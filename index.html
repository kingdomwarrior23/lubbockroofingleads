Common Questions from Lubbock Homeowners

After a hail event, look for circular dents on metal vents, gutters, and AC units — if those are dented, your shingles took hits too. On asphalt shingles, hail damage appears as dark spots where granules were knocked loose, leaving bare asphalt exposed. Most licensed Lubbock roofers offer free post-storm inspections and will document damage for your insurance adjuster at no charge.
Most standard Texas homeowners insurance policies cover hail damage under the "windstorm and hail" peril. However, many insurers now offer Actual Cash Value (ACV) policies rather than Replacement Cost Value (RCV), which deduct depreciation before paying out. Given Lubbock's hail frequency, some insurers now require Class 4 impact-resistant shingles to offer full RCV coverage. Always confirm your policy type before filing a claim.
A full residential roof replacement in Lubbock typically runs $8,000–$18,000 depending on material and home size. Standard 3-tab asphalt shingles cost $8,000–$12,000 installed. Class 4 impact-resistant architectural shingles run $12,000–$18,000 but can qualify you for insurance discounts and are increasingly required for full replacement coverage in high-hail markets like Lubbock.
Most standard Lubbock homes can be fully re-roofed in one to two days, weather permitting. After a major hail event, local contractors book out quickly — sometimes 2–4 weeks out. If your roof is actively leaking, ask about emergency tarping, which most Lubbock roofers can do within hours of your call.
