# Lubbock Hail Damage Roof Claims: A Homeowner's Guide

Lubbock had 47 documented hail events in 2025 alone, with the June 5 storm producing baseball-sized hail and NWS confirming "most of the roofs are totaled" in parts of southwest Lubbock. If your home was in the path of a storm, here's exactly what to do — in order.

## Step 1: Document Everything Within 48 Hours

Before anyone touches your roof, document the condition. Walk around the exterior and photograph:

- All sides of the roof from the ground
- Close-ups of any visibly damaged shingles
- Dented gutters, downspouts, and metal fascia
- Dented AC unit fins and caps
- Any damaged skylight covers or vent caps

The dented metal components matter because they're objective — a hail impact that dents a gutter or AC cap left the same force on adjacent shingles, even if shingle damage isn't immediately obvious from the ground.

## Step 2: Get a Roofer's Inspection Before Your Adjuster Visits

This step is where most Lubbock homeowners leave money on the table. Insurance adjusters — particularly for large storms where they're handling dozens of claims — sometimes undercount impact marks or miss flashing damage. Having an independent inspection report from a licensed Lubbock roofer gives you:

- Documentation of every impact mark per roof section
- Photographs of granule loss patterns
- A professional assessment of whether damage meets the threshold for replacement vs. repair
- An advocate who can meet with your adjuster and point out what they're missing

Most legitimate Lubbock roofers do this inspection free of charge.

## Step 3: File Your Claim Promptly

Texas law gives you one year from the date of damage to file a claim under most standard homeowners policies. But waiting is a mistake. Adjusters become skeptical that damage is pre-existing when claims come in months after a storm. File within 30 days.

When you call, have:
- The date of the storm
- Your policy number
- The photo documentation you gathered
- The roofer's inspection report if you have it

## Step 4: Understand Your Policy Type Before Your Adjuster Arrives

This is critical. Request your declarations page and look for the roofing section under "special provisions" or "endorsements." Two types:

**RCV (Replacement Cost Value):** You receive the full cost to replace the roof with equivalent materials, minus your deductible. This is the gold standard.

**ACV (Actual Cash Value):** The insurer deducts depreciation before paying. On a 10-year-old roof, this can mean receiving 40–60% of replacement cost. Many Lubbock policies have shifted to ACV due to the area's hail frequency.

If you have ACV and your roof is over 10 years old, ask your agent about endorsements that convert it to RCV — often called "Roof Replacement Cost Coverage" or similar.

## Step 5: What Happens If You Disagree with the Adjuster's Assessment

You have options. First, have your roofer's documentation ready to present. If the adjuster's estimate is still lower than the actual damage, you can request a re-inspection or invoke the appraisal clause most Texas policies include — each party selects an appraiser, those two appoint an umpire, and the umpire's decision is binding.

For significant discrepancies, a licensed public adjuster can handle the claim on your behalf for a percentage of the settlement.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How long do I have to file a hail damage claim in Texas?**
Texas law allows one year from the date of damage under most standard policies. But waiting increases the risk your damage will be disputed as pre-existing. File within 30 days of a storm.

**Should I get a roofer's inspection before calling my insurance in Lubbock?**
Yes. A licensed Lubbock roofer documents damage with photos and measurements before your adjuster visits, giving you an independent assessment and preventing undercounting.

**What does ACV vs RCV mean for my Lubbock roof claim?**
ACV deducts depreciation from your payout. RCV pays the full replacement cost minus your deductible. Check your declarations page — many Lubbock policies have shifted to ACV for roofs.
