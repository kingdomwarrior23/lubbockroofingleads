# Best Roofing Companies in Lubbock TX: What to Look For

Lubbock gets hit hard by hail — 306 documented events near the city center since 2004, with the most recent significant damage in June 2025 when NWS confirmed totaled roofs in southwest Lubbock near 103rd and Winston. After every major storm, the metro fills with roofing contractors. Some are excellent. Some are out-of-state storm chasers who won't be here to honor a warranty. Here's how to tell the difference.

## What Separates a Good Lubbock Roofer From a Bad One

The single most reliable signal is local history. A Lubbock-based contractor who's been operating in the 806 for 5+ years has a reputation to protect, a local license to lose, and neighbors who can verify their work. Storm chasers from out of state have none of those accountability structures.

## The Non-Negotiables: What Every Lubbock Roofer Must Have

**Texas contractor licensing:** Roofing contractors in Texas must register with the Texas Department of Licensing and Regulation (TDLR). Ask for their TDLR registration number and verify it at tdlr.texas.gov. This takes two minutes and filters out a large percentage of fly-by-night operations.

**Liability insurance:** Minimum $300,000 general liability. Ask for a certificate of insurance naming you as the certificate holder. This protects you if a worker damages your property or a shingle blows off during a storm.

**Workers' compensation:** If a roofer falls from your roof and doesn't have workers' comp, you could be liable. Most legitimate Lubbock contractors carry it.

**Local address:** An actual Lubbock address, not a PO box. You should be able to show up if there's a warranty issue.

## Questions to Ask Before Signing Anything

- "How long has your company been operating in Lubbock specifically?"
- "Can you give me two references in my zip code from the last 12 months?"
- "What shingle manufacturer warranty comes with this installation, and are you a certified installer?"
- "Will you meet with my insurance adjuster directly, or do I need to handle that myself?"
- "What's your written warranty on workmanship, and what does it cover?"

## Red Flags to Watch For After a Lubbock Storm

**"We'll waive your deductible."** This is insurance fraud in Texas and is explicitly illegal under Texas Insurance Code. Any contractor offering this is either doing it by padding the claim or by cutting corners on materials.

**Demanding full payment upfront.** A deposit of 10–30% to order materials is standard. Full payment before work starts is a red flag.

**No written scope of work.** Every legitimate contractor provides a written estimate that itemizes: tear-off and disposal, deck inspection and repair, underlayment, drip edge, flashing, shingles, and ridge cap. If any of these are missing from an estimate, ask why.

**Pressure to sign immediately.** "This price is only good today." Post-storm urgency is real, but a legitimate contractor won't pressure you to sign without letting you get competing estimates.

## What a Legitimate Inspection Looks Like

A real inspection takes 20–45 minutes. The contractor goes on the roof, checks every field of shingles for granule loss and impact marks, inspects all flashing, checks ridge caps, looks at gutters and downspouts for granule accumulation, and inspects any skylights or penetrations. They should take photos throughout.

If a contractor gives you a quote after a 5-minute walkthrough from the driveway, that's not an inspection.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How do I find a licensed roofing contractor in Lubbock TX?**
Verify Texas contractor license through TDLR (tdlr.texas.gov). Ask for proof of liability insurance and workers' comp. Local Lubbock roofers with years of history here are accountable in ways out-of-state storm chasers are not.

**What are red flags when hiring a Lubbock roofer?**
Full payment required upfront, no written estimate, no Texas license number, no local address, pressure to sign immediately, and offers to "waive your deductible" (illegal under Texas Insurance Code).

**How many estimates should I get for a Lubbock roof replacement?**
At least three written estimates within the first week after storm damage. All should itemize materials, labor, tear-off, and disposal separately.
